History

Yashwantrao Chavan Maharashtra Open University (YCMOU) was established on 1 st. July 1989 by an Act (XX) of (1989) of the Maharashtra State Legislature. It is the fifth Open University in India. The jurisdiction of this university is spread over the entire state of Maharashtra. YCMOU has been recognized under section 12 (B) of the University Grants Commission, New Delhi. The university is a member of various prestigious associations and academic bodies like the Association of Indian Universities (AIU), Association of Commonwealth Universities (ACU), Asian Association of Open Universities (AAOU), International Council for Open and Distance Education (ICDE) to name a few. The goal of the university is to become a Mass Varsity, reaching out to large sections of population through education and extension programs in general education, technical education and professional education which provide better prospects for the future. The University has designed and developed many academic and extension programs for diversified group of learners including farmers, rural and tribal communities.

Vission

To provide lifelong learning opportunities for all, democratise higher education and become a mass university

Mission

Through our technical, vocational, professional and liberal education programmes, application of modern communication technologies and adoption of the distance education methodology, we strive towards developing an Innovative, Flexible and Open system of education.

Core Values

Access :- The University provides acces to higher education to all sections of the society by cutting across are, caste, class, gender, region, ethnicity, language and religion.

Affordability :- The University offers higher education at affordable costs to learners who could not access education due to high costs and lack of resources.

Employability :- The academic programs are customised to suit the neds of employer and to increase employability of program to suit the professional world.

Flexibility :- The University provides academic lexibility by removing barrirs to entry, place and pace of learning through seamless teaching-learning experices using aproopriate education technologies.

Awards given by YCMOU

Vishakha Kavya Puraskar: The award named after the poetic composition of the noted poet laureate and winner of the prestigious Dnyanpeeth Award, Shri V.V. Shirwadkar (popularly known as ‘Tatyasaheb’) is given every year to the best three maiden poetic compositions.

Baburao Bagul Gaurav Puraskar: In recognition of the noted contributions of Shri Baburao Bagul to the field of Marathi literature, an award has been instituted in his name and is given annually to the maiden story writing productions of a budding short-story writer.

Rukmini Puraskar: This award is given to any woman who has made outstanding contributions in a given field, particularly in the area of Social Work.
